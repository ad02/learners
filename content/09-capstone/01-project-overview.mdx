# Your Final Project

Congratulations — you made it to the capstone!

Over the last eight modules, you went from zero to knowing how to use VS Code, Git, AI prompting, Claude Code, web basics, WordPress, APIs, and automation workflows. That is a LOT.

Now it is time to put it all together. You are going to build a **complete automation** from start to finish — a real workflow that connects multiple tools into one seamless process.

## How This Works

You will pick **one of three project templates** below. Each template is a guided, step-by-step walkthrough. You will not be left on your own — every step tells you exactly what to do and why.

Think of it like following a recipe. You have already learned what each ingredient does. Now you are combining them into a full dish.

## Choose Your Template

### Template A: Blog Auto-Publisher

**What it does:** When someone submits a form, AI summarizes the content, publishes it as a WordPress draft, and notifies your team on Slack.

**Skills used:** Webhooks (Module 7), AI prompting (Module 3 & 4), WordPress REST API (Module 6 & 7), Notifications (Module 8)

**Best for:** People who want to automate content publishing. Great if you manage a blog or content website.

---

### Template B: Daily Digest Bot

**What it does:** Every day at 9am, it fetches data from an API, uses AI to create a summary, posts it to WordPress, and commits the config to GitHub.

**Skills used:** Scheduled triggers (Module 8), APIs (Module 7), AI prompting (Module 3 & 4), WordPress (Module 6), Git/GitHub (Module 2)

**Best for:** People who want to automate daily reports or news digests. Great if you work with data or analytics.

---

### Template C: Support Ticket Router

**What it does:** When a support email arrives, AI categorizes it, updates a WordPress tracking page, and notifies the right team member.

**Skills used:** Webhooks (Module 7), AI classification (Module 3 & 4), WordPress REST API (Module 6 & 7), Notifications (Module 8)

**Best for:** People who want to automate customer support workflows. Great if you handle support tickets or customer emails.

## How to Choose Your Template

Not sure which one to pick? Here is a guide based on your role or interests:

| Your Role / Interest | Recommended Template |
|---|---|
| Content creator, blogger, marketing | **Template A** — Blog Auto-Publisher |
| Data analyst, operations, reporting | **Template B** — Daily Digest Bot |
| Customer support, account management | **Template C** — Support Ticket Router |
| "I just want the simplest one" | **Template A** — fewest steps |
| "I want the most challenge" | **Template B** — uses the most tools |

## What "Done" Looks Like

Here is what a completed project looks like for each template:

**Template A — Done means:**
- A webhook URL is set up and receiving data
- AI processes incoming submissions and generates a summary
- WordPress drafts are created automatically with the right title, content, and category
- Slack notifications arrive within seconds of a form submission
- You have tested it end to end with at least 3 test submissions

**Template B — Done means:**
- A scheduled trigger fires daily (or you can trigger it manually for testing)
- Live data is fetched from an API
- AI generates a readable daily summary
- A WordPress post is published with the digest
- The workflow config is committed to GitHub
- You have tested it with at least 2 runs

**Template C — Done means:**
- A webhook captures incoming email data
- AI correctly categorizes and assigns tickets
- WordPress tracking entries are created with all relevant details
- The right team member gets notified based on the category
- You have tested it with at least 3 different ticket types (billing, technical, account)

## Timeline Suggestion

Plan to spend **1-2 hours** on your capstone project. Here is a rough breakdown:

- **15 minutes** — Read through all the steps before starting
- **30 minutes** — Set up the trigger and AI step
- **30 minutes** — Add the WordPress and notification steps
- **15 minutes** — Test everything end to end and fix any issues
- **Optional: 15 minutes** — Polish and add extras (better prompts, error handling)

You do not need to finish in one sitting. It is totally fine to take a break and come back. Your automation tool saves your progress.
