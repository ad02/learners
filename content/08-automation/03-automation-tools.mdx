# Automation Tools: n8n, Make & Zapier

Now that you understand triggers and actions, you need a tool to actually **build** your automations. The good news? You don't need to write any code.

There are three popular tools that let you build automations by **dragging and dropping** blocks on a screen. Think of them like LEGO — you snap pieces together to build what you want.

## The Big Three

### Zapier — The Easy One

**Zapier** (rhymes with "happier") is the most beginner-friendly automation tool.

- **Best for:** People who want the simplest experience
- **How it works:** Pick a trigger, pick an action, done
- **Integrations:** Connects to 6,000+ apps (the most of any tool)
- **Cost:** Free plan available, but paid plans for most useful features
- **Downside:** Can get expensive as you add more automations

Think of Zapier like an **automatic car** — easy to drive, but you have less control over what's happening under the hood.

### Make (formerly Integromat) — The Visual One

**Make** is more powerful than Zapier and has a beautiful visual interface where you can see your entire workflow as a diagram.

- **Best for:** People who want more control and a visual approach
- **How it works:** Drag and drop modules on a canvas, connect them with lines
- **Integrations:** Connects to 1,500+ apps
- **Cost:** Generous free plan (1,000 operations per month)
- **Downside:** Slightly steeper learning curve than Zapier

Think of Make like a **manual car** — takes a bit more learning, but gives you more control and is often more fun to drive.

### n8n — The Open Source One

**n8n** (pronounced "n-eight-n") is the most flexible option because it's **open source** — meaning the code is free and you can run it on your own computer or server.

- **Best for:** People who want full control and don't mind a bit of setup
- **How it works:** Visual workflow editor, similar to Make
- **Integrations:** Connects to 400+ apps, plus you can build custom ones
- **Cost:** Completely free if you self-host
- **Downside:** Requires some technical setup to get started

Think of n8n like **building your own car** — the most work upfront, but you can customize everything exactly how you want.

## Detailed Comparison

| Feature | Zapier | Make | n8n |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Free plan** | 100 tasks/month | 1,000 ops/month | Unlimited (self-hosted) |
| **Paid plans start at** | $20/month | $9/month | $20/month (cloud) |
| **Number of integrations** | 6,000+ | 1,500+ | 400+ (but growing fast) |
| **Visual workflow editor** | Linear (step by step) | Canvas (drag anywhere) | Canvas (drag anywhere) |
| **Conditional logic** | Basic (Paths) | Advanced (Routers) | Advanced (IF nodes) |

| **AI integrations** | Good | Good | Excellent |
| **Self-hosting option** | No | No | Yes (free!) |
| **Learning curve** | Easiest | Moderate | Moderate-Hard |
| **Best for** | Quick simple automations | Visual, complex workflows | Full control, technical users |

## When to Use Each Tool

Here is a decision guide based on your situation:

**Choose Zapier if:**
- You want the fastest setup possible
- You need to connect two specific apps and nothing else
- You don't mind paying for convenience
- You are brand new to automation

**Choose Make if:**
- You want a good balance of power and ease
- You plan to build workflows with multiple branches
- You want a generous free plan
- You like visual, diagram-style editors

**Choose n8n if:**
- You want to learn how automation really works under the hood
- You care about data privacy (your data stays on your server)
- You want to use AI models without per-request fees
- You enjoy tinkering and customizing

## Self-Hosted vs. Cloud

Two of these tools (n8n and Make) offer different hosting options. Here is what that means:

**Cloud-hosted** (Zapier, Make cloud, n8n cloud):
- Someone else runs the servers for you
- You just log in and use it
- Pros: no setup, always available, automatic updates
- Cons: monthly fees, your data is on their servers

**Self-hosted** (n8n only):
- You run the software on your own computer or server
- You control everything
- Pros: free, private, fully customizable
- Cons: you handle setup, updates, and backups

For beginners, cloud is the way to go. Self-hosting is great once you are more comfortable with the tools.

## Why n8n is Great for Learning

While we recommend starting with Make for its balance, n8n deserves a special mention for learning:

- **It is free.** No task limits, no feature gates. You can build as many workflows as you want.
- **It is visual.** You see every step, every piece of data, every transformation.
- **It shows you the data.** After each step runs, you can inspect exactly what data went in and what came out. This is incredibly helpful for understanding how APIs and data transformations work.
- **It supports AI natively.** n8n has built-in nodes for Claude, ChatGPT, and other AI models.
- **The community is active.** Thousands of free workflow templates you can import and learn from.

## They All Work the Same Way

Despite their differences, all three tools follow the same pattern you already learned:

1. **Pick a trigger** — choose what starts your automation
2. **Add actions** — choose what happens next
3. **Connect them** — draw lines between the steps
4. **Test it** — run it once to make sure it works
5. **Turn it on** — let it run automatically from now on

## Try It Yourself

Let's get hands-on. Do this right now:

1. Go to [n8n.io](https://n8n.io) and sign up for a free cloud account
2. Once logged in, click "New Workflow"
3. Look around the interface — notice the canvas, the node panel on the left, and the execution controls at the top
4. Click the "+" button to see all available trigger and action nodes
5. Try adding a "Manual Trigger" node and a "Set" node, then connect them with a line
6. Click "Test Workflow" to see data flow through the steps

You do not need to build anything real yet. Just explore the interface and get comfortable with how it looks and feels.

## Don't Overthink It

The most important thing is to **just start building**. All three tools can do 90% of the same things. Pick one, learn it, and start automating.

You can always switch tools later. The concepts you learn (triggers, actions, workflows) work the same way everywhere.
