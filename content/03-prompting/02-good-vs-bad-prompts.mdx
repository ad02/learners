# Good vs Bad Prompts

The quality of what AI gives you depends entirely on **how you ask**. A vague question gets a vague answer. A specific question gets a useful answer.

Let's look at the difference.

## Example 1: Marketing Post

**Bad prompt:**
> "Write something about marketing"

This is like walking into a restaurant and saying "food please." You'll get *something*, but probably not what you wanted.

**Good prompt:**
> "You are a marketing expert. Write a 200-word LinkedIn post about why small businesses should use automation. Use a professional but friendly tone."

See the difference? The good prompt tells AI **who to be**, **what to write**, **how long**, and **what tone**. The result will be something you can actually use.

## Example 2: Email Writing

**Bad prompt:**
> "Write an email"

**Good prompt:**
> "Write a short email to a client named Sarah. Thank her for the meeting yesterday and confirm our next call is Tuesday at 2pm. Keep it warm and professional."

The good version gives AI **context** (who, what happened) and **format** (short, warm, professional).

## Example 3: Summarizing

**Bad prompt:**
> "Summarize this"

**Good prompt:**
> "Summarize this article in 5 bullet points. Focus on the main takeaways a business owner would care about."

The good version tells AI **how** to summarize (bullet points) and **for whom** (a business owner).

## Example 4: Problem Solving

**Bad prompt:**
> "Help me with my project"

**Good prompt:**
> "I'm planning a team offsite for 15 people in Austin, Texas, in June. Our budget is $5,000. Suggest 3 venue options with pros and cons for each."

The good version gives AI **specific details** so it can give you **specific answers**.

## What Makes a Prompt Good?

Every good prompt has three things:

1. **Specificity** — tell AI exactly what you want, not something general
2. **Context** — give background so AI understands the situation
3. **Format** — describe how you want the answer to look
