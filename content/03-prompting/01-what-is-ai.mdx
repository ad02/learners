# What is AI?

Imagine you just hired a **very smart intern**. This intern has read millions of books, articles, and websites. They can write, summarize, translate, and even code. But here's the catch — they only do exactly what you tell them.

That's AI in a nutshell.

## AI is Not Magic

AI doesn't "think" the way you do. It doesn't have feelings, opinions, or common sense. Instead, it's really good at **following instructions and finding patterns**.

Think of it like a calculator on steroids. A calculator doesn't "understand" math — it just follows rules really fast. AI does the same thing, but with words and ideas instead of numbers.

### What AI Can't Do

It's just as important to know what AI **can't** do:

- It **doesn't "know" things** — it predicts what words come next based on patterns it learned from text
- It **can be wrong** — confidently. It might give you a completely made-up fact and present it as truth (this is called a "hallucination")
- It **doesn't learn from your conversations** — each conversation starts fresh (unless the tool specifically saves your chat history)
- It **doesn't have real-time information** — it was trained on data up to a certain date and doesn't know what happened yesterday unless it has internet access
- It **can't take actions on its own** — it can't send emails, make purchases, or access your files unless connected to other tools

Understanding these limits will save you from trusting AI blindly. Always double-check important facts.

## How is AI Different from Regular Software?

Regular software follows **fixed rules**. When you type a search into Google, it matches your words against a database. It does the same thing every time.

AI is different. It **adapts to your instructions**. You can ask it to write a formal email, then ask it to rewrite that same email in a casual tone. It adjusts based on what you tell it.

Here's a simple comparison:

- **Regular software:** "Search for 'marketing tips'" → shows a list of links
- **AI:** "Write me 5 marketing tips for a bakery in a friendly tone" → writes original content for you

## AI You Already Use Every Day

You might not realize it, but you're already using AI regularly:

- **Autocomplete on your phone** — when your keyboard suggests the next word, that's AI predicting what you'll type
- **Spam filters in email** — Gmail and Outlook use AI to decide which emails are spam
- **Netflix/YouTube recommendations** — "You watched X, so you might like Y" is AI finding patterns in your behavior
- **Google Maps traffic predictions** — AI analyzes millions of trips to predict how long your drive will take
- **Smart assistants** — Siri, Alexa, and Google Assistant all use AI to understand your voice

The AI tools we'll learn in this course are different because you can **have conversations** with them and give them complex instructions. But the underlying idea is the same: pattern recognition and prediction.

## What Can AI Actually Do?

AI can help with tasks you do every day:

- **Write** emails, reports, social media posts, marketing copy
- **Summarize** long documents into key points
- **Translate** text between languages
- **Brainstorm** ideas for projects or campaigns
- **Explain** complicated topics in simple words
- **Organize** messy notes into clean lists or tables
- **Analyze** data and spot patterns
- **Create** templates and workflows you can reuse
- **Debug** problems by explaining error messages in plain English

## Why Does This Matter for Your Work?

Learning to use AI is like learning to use email 20 years ago. Back then, people thought email was optional. Now imagine doing your job without it.

AI is the same kind of shift. The people who learn to use it well will **save hours every week** on tasks that used to take forever.

Here are real examples of time savings:

| Task | Without AI | With AI |
|------|-----------|---------|
| Write a client email | 15 minutes | 2 minutes |
| Summarize a 10-page report | 30 minutes | 1 minute |
| Create 5 social media posts | 1 hour | 10 minutes |
| Research a new topic | 2 hours | 20 minutes |
| Write a job description | 45 minutes | 5 minutes |

The good news? You don't need to be technical. You just need to learn how to **talk to it clearly**. That's exactly what this module will teach you.

## Try It Yourself

Let's have your first AI conversation:

1. Go to **[claude.ai](https://claude.ai)** in your browser
2. Create a free account (or sign in if you have one)
3. In the chat box, type: **"Explain what AI is in 3 sentences, as if you're talking to someone who has never used a computer before."**
4. Read the response. Then try: **"Now explain it as if you're talking to a 10-year-old."**

Notice how the AI adapts its language based on your instructions? That's the power of prompting — and you just did it for the first time!

## Practice Scenario

Think about your current job. Pick one task you do repeatedly (answering emails, writing reports, organizing information). Now ask yourself:

1. Could AI help with this task?
2. What information would AI need to do it well?
3. What would you still need to check or edit yourself?

Write down your answers. This kind of thinking — identifying where AI fits into your work — is the most valuable skill you'll develop in this course.
