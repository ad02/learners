# What is WordPress?

Here's a fun fact: **about 40% of all websites on the internet run on WordPress**. That means nearly half the websites you visit every day — from news sites to online stores — are powered by this one tool.

## So what exactly is WordPress?

Think of WordPress like a **pre-built house kit**. The structure, walls, and roof are already designed for you. Your job? Pick the paint color, arrange the furniture, and make it yours. You don't need to know how to pour concrete or wire electricity.

WordPress is what's called a **CMS** — a **Content Management System**. That's just a fancy way of saying "a tool that lets you create and edit a website without writing code."

## WordPress.com vs WordPress.org

This confuses almost everyone, so let's clear it up:

### WordPress.com — The Easy Route
- It's a **hosted service** — they handle the technical stuff (server, updates, security)
- You sign up, pick a template, and start building
- Free plan available, but with limits (ads on your site, can't install custom plugins)
- Think of it like **renting a furnished apartment** — convenient, but you can't knock down walls

### WordPress.org — The Full Power
- It's the **free software** you download and install on your own hosting
- Full control — install any theme, any plugin, edit any code
- You're responsible for updates, backups, and security (or your hosting company handles it)
- Think of it like **owning your own house** — more work, but total freedom

For most professional work, people use **WordPress.org** — and that's what we'll focus on in this module.

## Who Uses WordPress?

All kinds of people and organizations:

- **Bloggers** — sharing recipes, travel stories, or personal journals
- **Small businesses** — showing off services, hours, and contact info
- **Online stores** — selling products with shopping carts and checkout
- **News sites** — publishing articles and breaking stories
- **Portfolios** — designers and photographers showcasing their work

## Famous Sites That Use WordPress

You might be surprised at how many big names run on WordPress:

- **The White House** (whitehouse.gov) — the official US government site
- **TechCrunch** — one of the biggest tech news sites
- **BBC America** — major media network
- **Sony Music** — global entertainment company
- **The New Yorker** — legendary magazine

If WordPress is good enough for the White House, it can handle your project!

## The WordPress Ecosystem

WordPress isn't just software — it's a massive community and marketplace:

### Themes
- Thousands of pre-made designs, both free and premium ($30-100)
- One click to change your entire site's look
- Customizable without touching code

### Plugins
- Over 60,000 free plugins in the official directory
- Add any feature you can imagine: forms, SEO, e-commerce, membership sites, booking systems
- Install and activate with a few clicks

### Community
- Millions of developers, designers, and users worldwide
- WordCamp conferences in cities around the world
- Free support forums with answers to almost any question
- Thousands of tutorials, courses, and YouTube videos

## Why WordPress Is Great for Automation

Here's why WordPress matters for your automation journey:

- **REST API** — WordPress has a built-in API that lets other tools read and write content. You can create posts, update pages, and manage users automatically.
- **Webhooks** — trigger automations when things happen on your site (new post published, form submitted, order placed).
- **Plugin integrations** — many plugins connect to tools like Zapier, Make, and n8n for no-code automation.
- **WP-CLI** — a command-line tool that lets you manage WordPress from the terminal (perfect with Claude Code!).
- **Huge market** — so many businesses use WordPress that knowing how to automate it makes you incredibly valuable.

## Why This Matters

Understanding WordPress means understanding the platform that runs nearly half the web. Whether you're building your own site or automating tasks for clients, WordPress skills are immediately practical.

## Tips and Common Mistakes

- **Don't confuse .com and .org** — if someone asks you to work on their WordPress site, they almost certainly mean WordPress.org (self-hosted).
- **WordPress is free, but not free** — the software costs nothing, but you still need to pay for hosting and a domain name.
- **It's not just for blogs** — WordPress started as blogging software, but today it powers every type of website imaginable.
- **Updates matter** — keeping WordPress, themes, and plugins updated is critical for security.

## Practice Scenario

Take a look at three websites you visit regularly. Can you tell if they use WordPress? Here are two quick ways to check:

1. **Add `/wp-admin` to the URL** — if you see a login page, it's WordPress (e.g., `example.com/wp-admin`)
2. **View the page source** — search for "wp-content" in the code. If you find it, the site runs on WordPress.

You might be surprised how many sites you'll find! This is a fun detective exercise that reinforces what you've learned.

Don't worry about the technical details right now. The important thing to know is that WordPress lets anyone — yes, even complete beginners — build a real, professional-looking website.
