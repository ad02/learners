# Pages and Posts

WordPress has two main types of content: **Pages** and **Posts**. They might seem similar, but they serve very different purposes.

## Pages: Your Permanent Content

**Pages** are for content that doesn't change much. Think of them as the rooms in your house — they stay where they are.

Examples of pages:

- **About** — who you are and what you do
- **Contact** — how people can reach you
- **Services** — what you offer
- **Privacy Policy** — legal stuff

Pages don't have dates or categories. They just sit there, quietly doing their job.

## Posts: Your Fresh Content

**Posts** are for content that's timely — things you publish regularly, like a blog. Think of them as letters you send out. Each one has a date, and they appear in order from newest to oldest.

Examples of posts:

- Blog articles
- Company news and announcements
- Tips and tutorials
- Product updates

Posts can be organized with **categories** (broad topics like "Marketing" or "News") and **tags** (specific keywords like "email" or "social media").

## Creating a Page or Post

The process is almost the same for both:

1. Go to **Pages** or **Posts** in the sidebar
2. Click **"Add New"**
3. Type your **title** at the top
4. Write your **content** below
5. Click **"Publish"** when you're ready

It's really that simple.

## The Block Editor (Gutenberg)

WordPress uses something called the **Block Editor** (also known as Gutenberg). Instead of typing everything into one big text box, you build your content with **blocks** — like snapping together LEGO pieces.

Common blocks you'll use:

- **Paragraph** — regular text (this is the default)
- **Heading** — section titles (H2, H3, etc.)
- **Image** — add a photo or graphic
- **List** — bullet points or numbered lists
- **Button** — a clickable call-to-action
- **Columns** — split content side by side

To add a new block, click the **"+"** button and search for the type you want. You can drag blocks around to rearrange them.

## The Media Library

When you add an image block, you can either:

- **Upload** a new image from your computer
- **Choose from the Media Library** — a collection of all images and files you've already uploaded

The Media Library is like a filing cabinet for all your images, PDFs, and documents. Everything you upload lives there and can be reused across multiple pages and posts.

A quick tip: before uploading images, make sure they're not too large. Images over 2MB can slow your site down. A good size is usually under 500KB.
